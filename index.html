Expectations.

Future Computer Engineer

In Progress...

_

One of my expecations for the work immersion was, this was the start I needed to further my dream of becoming a computer engineer. It would help me answer certain questions about the work related to the technology field. I expected to be in a professional scenario, wherein we will actually work as interns. Another thing I was expecting was that we would be able to learn a lot about the different jobs the information technology can produce. As well as tips and tricks by the immersion supervisor, things we should take into account if we are going to apply for a job in the technological world.

People Encountered.

Immersion Supervisor: Miss Jennifer Valerio

I've met a ton of people from the different schools in Batch 2 of the External Immersion. (Justice Cecilia Muñoz Palma Senior High School, Holy Trinity Academy, Don Alejandro Roces Sr. Science-Technology High School, New Era High School, and ofcourse, The Nazarene Catholic School). Apart from the students, I've also met the people handling the immersion apart from our imemrsion adviser as well as Ms. Audrey Roxette Pedrosa. I'm thankful for this work immersion as I've had the opportunity to make new friends during the time of the immersion. I've also seen the glimpse of what kind of people the future of the IT field has to offer.

I was informed that other immersion departments use Google Meet as there place to learn, while we use Zoom for our meetings. We have three sections because the department had too much students to handle. But, even if we have different sections we all join the same zoom immersion. So, I met every student aspiring to pursue a course in the IT field. Our immersion supervisor didn't require us to open our cameras and focused more on our paticipation during her lectures. So, naturally we seldomly view the other students' faces.

Learnings.

There were tons of webinar sessions with our immersion supervisor. Apart from the questions we ask from time to time during synchronous sessions, there was snippets of her tips as a professional working in the IT Field. She gave us great tips during our time with her. She even showed to us different intern opportunities, events and webinars outside of the immersion and other opportunities we couldn't miss as an aspiring IT.

Realizations.

As the immersion had finished, I've realized a lot of stuff. It gave me both fear and courage to start working.
It hit me in the head, work isn't that easy, it's something that I should take seriously, otherwise, I would be
swallowed by it. So I must ready myself both physically and mentally before working. Other realizations include:

The expecations I had before the work immersion had been fulfilled.

I've found a new motivation to pursue the computer engineering course.

The job I want to pursue has tons of obstacles that I'd need to face.

Life isn't always fair, I have to be brave and never give up in chasing my dream.

The information technology field is flexible in terms of job choices.
